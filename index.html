Why I love the Premier League

Football has been my favourite hobby for as long as I can remember, and the English Premier League is the competition that excites me the most. I love the fast tempo, the passionate fans and the feeling that anything can happen in the final minutes of a game. Every weekend I look forward to watching the highlights, checking the table and talking about the big moments with friends and family. The drama, the rivalries and the atmosphere make it feel more like a story than just a sport.

My favourite Premier League club

My favourite team is Chelsea. I first fell in love with them during the 2005/2006 Premier League season, when José Mourinho led the club to their second consecutive league title. Something about that team, the confidence, the solid defence and the electric atmosphere at Stamford Bridge pulled me in instantly. From that moment, watching Chelsea became more than just football; it turned into a weekly ritual. Over the years, I have enjoyed learning about the club’s rich history, iconic kits, unforgettable matches and legendary players who shaped each era. I try to follow every game I can and always keep an eye on transfer news and young academy talents coming through the ranks. Supporting Chelsea makes every season feel like a new chapter in a story I’ve been part of for almost two decades.

My matchday routine

When my team plays, I like to turn the day into a small ritual. These are the usual steps I follow:

  1. Check the early team news, injuries and predicted line-ups.
  2. Prepare a drink and a simple snack before kick-off.
  3. Watch the match live if possible, or follow a live commentary.
  4. Discuss key moments with friends on WhatsApp or social media.
  5. After the game, look at the updated league table and statistics.
